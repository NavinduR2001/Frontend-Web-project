ANURADHAPURA

Explore Anuradhapura & Polonnaruwa: Ancient Capitals of Sri Lanka

Welcome to the ancient cities of Anuradhapura and Polonnaruwa, where history comes alive amidst magnificent ruins and sacred relics. These UNESCO World Heritage Sites showcase Sri Lanka's rich cultural heritage and architectural marvels from centuries past.

Anuradhapura
Step into the cradle of Sri Lanka's civilization at Anuradhapura, one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world. Marvel at the towering dagobas (stupas) such as Ruwanwelisaya and Jetavanaramaya, which stand as testaments to ancient engineering prowess and religious devotion. Explore the sacred Bodhi Tree, believed to be the oldest historically authenticated tree in the world, and a symbol of enlightenment.

Polonnaruwa
Travel back in time to the medieval capital of Polonnaruwa, where grand palaces, intricately carved temples, and colossal Buddha statues await. Discover the imposing Gal Vihara, a rock temple adorned with four exquisite Buddha statues carved from a single granite rock. Explore the Royal Palace complex and the stunning Parakrama Samudra, an ancient reservoir that highlights the engineering prowess of the era.

Natural Splendor
Surrounded by lush greenery and serene lakes, the Anuradhapura and Polonnaruwa areas offer more than just ancient ruins. Take a leisurely stroll through landscaped gardens and enjoy birdwatching at tranquil lakeshores. Discover the peacefulness of rural Sri Lanka as you explore quaint villages and interact with friendly locals.

Cultural Treasures
Experience the vibrant cultural traditions of Sri Lanka through colorful festivals, traditional dance performances, and local craftsmanship. Sample authentic Sri Lankan cuisine at local eateries, where spicy curries and flavorful rice dishes await.

Sacred Pilgrimage
For spiritual seekers, the Anuradhapura and Polonnaruwa areas offer a profound journey of pilgrimage and reflection. Visit sacred temples, participate in rituals, and witness the spiritual devotion of local worshippers.

Adventure Awaits
From cycling tours through ancient ruins to wildlife safaris in nearby national parks, there's plenty of adventure to be had in this historically and ecologically rich region.

Explore the timeless beauty and cultural grandeur of Anuradhapura and Polonnaruwa, where every step unveils a piece of Sri Lanka's illustrious past and vibrant present.

Anuradhapura

Location

Anuradhapura is a city located in the North Central Province of Sri Lanka, approximately 205 kilometers (127 miles) north of Colombo. It is one of the ancient capitals of Sri Lanka and holds great historical and cultural significance.

Historical Significance

Anuradhapura was the capital of Sri Lanka from the 4th century BC to the 11th century AD and is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world. It was a center of Theravada Buddhism and a hub of ancient civilization, with remarkable architectural achievements such as stupas, monasteries, and reservoirs.

Tourism Infrastructure

  • Accommodation: Anuradhapura offers a range of accommodation options, including hotels, guesthouses, and resorts catering to various budgets.
  • Dining: The city has a variety of dining options, ranging from local Sri Lankan cuisine to international dishes.
  • Activities: Visitors can explore ancient ruins, temples, museums, and enjoy cultural performances and ceremonies.

Places for travel

Sri Maha Bodhi, Ruwanwelisaya, Jetavanaramaya, Abhayagiri Monastery, Isurumuniya, Anuradhapura Museum.

Polonnaruwa

Location

Polonnaruwa is a city located in the North Central Province of Sri Lanka, approximately 216 kilometers (134 miles) northeast of Colombo. It served as the second capital of ancient Sri Lanka after the decline of Anuradhapura.

Historical Significance

Polonnaruwa was the capital of Sri Lanka from the 11th to the 13th century AD during the reign of the Chola and Sinhalese kings. It is renowned for its well-preserved archaeological sites and cultural heritage, showcasing a blend of South Indian Chola architecture and Sinhalese Buddhist artistry.

Tourism Infrastructure

  • Accommodation:Polonnaruwa offers a range of accommodation options, including hotels, guesthouses, and eco-lodges catering to various budgets.
  • Dining: The city has several dining options, featuring local Sri Lankan cuisine as well as international dishes to cater to tourists.
  • Activities:Visitors can explore ancient ruins, temples, museums, and enjoy cultural performances and traditional ceremonies.

Places for travel

Ancient City of Polonnaruwa, Parakrama Samudra, Gal Vihara, Vatadage, Royal Palace of King Parakramabahu I, Archaeological Museum of Polonnaruwa.
