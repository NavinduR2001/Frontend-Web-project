KANDY

Discover Kandy, Sri Lanka's Cultural Capital

Welcome to Kandy, nestled amidst misty hills and lush greenery, where ancient traditions blend harmoniously with natural beauty. Known as Sri Lanka's cultural capital, Kandy offers a captivating glimpse into the island's rich heritage and spiritual essence.

Explore the iconic Temple of the Tooth Relic (Sri Dalada Maligawa), a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of Buddhism's holiest shrines. Witness traditional rituals and ceremonies that have been preserved for centuries, offering a profound insight into Sri Lanka's religious practices.

Immerse yourself in the serene ambiance of the Royal Botanical Gardens in Peradeniya, where exotic flora from around the world flourishes amidst landscaped grounds and serene pathways. Marvel at towering trees, vibrant orchids, and the calming waters of the Mahaweli River. Venture into the surrounding hills to discover quaint villages, tea plantations, and cascading waterfalls that dot the landscape. Embark on a scenic train journey through misty mountains, offering breathtaking views of rolling hills and picturesque valleys.

Indulge in the flavors of Kandy with its distinctive cuisine, featuring aromatic spices and fresh produce. From local delicacies to international fare, dining in Kandy is a culinary journey that satisfies all tastes. Experience the warmth and hospitality of the Kandyan people, known for their traditional dance and music performances that celebrate their cultural heritage. Discover handicrafts, batik art, and gemstones that showcase local craftsmanship and creativity. Whether you're exploring historical sites, enjoying nature's tranquility, or savoring culinary delights, Kandy promises a memorable journey filled with enchanting experiences and timeless charm.

Kandy

Location

Kandy is a major city located in the central part of Sri Lanka, approximately 115 kilometers (71 miles) northeast of Colombo. It is nestled amidst lush hills in the Kandy District of the Central Province.

Historical Significance

Kandy is renowned for its rich history and cultural heritage. It was the last capital of the ancient kings' era in Sri Lanka and is home to the Sacred Tooth Relic of the Buddha, housed in the Temple of the Tooth (Sri Dalada Maligawa). The city has UNESCO World Heritage status due to its significant cultural and religious importance.

Tourism Infrastructure

  • Accommodation: Kandy offers a range of accommodation options, from luxury hotels and boutique resorts to budget-friendly guesthouses and homestays.
  • Dining: The city boasts a diverse culinary scene, with options ranging from traditional Sri Lankan cuisine to international dishes, often with beautiful views of the surrounding hills.
  • Activities: Visitors can enjoy activities such as exploring historical and religious sites, scenic walks in botanical gardens, cultural performances, and shopping for handicrafts and souvenirs.

Places for travel

Temple of the Tooth (Sri Dalada Maligawa), Kandy Lake, Kandy View Point, Royal Botanical Gardens, Bahirawakanda Vihara Buddha Statue, Kandy Market.

Peradeniya

Location

Peradeniya is a suburb of Kandy city, located approximately 5 kilometers (3 miles) west of Kandy in the Central Province of Sri Lanka.

Historical Significance

Peradeniya is renowned for its historical significance primarily due to the Royal Botanical Gardens, which is one of the oldest and largest botanical gardens in Sri Lanka. Established during the British colonial era, the gardens served as a pleasure garden for Kandyan royalty before being developed into a botanical garden.

Tourism Infrastructure

  • Accommodation: While Peradeniya itself is primarily known for the botanical gardens, visitors typically stay in nearby Kandy, which offers a wide range of accommodation options.
  • Dining: Similarly, dining options in Peradeniya are limited, and visitors often choose to dine in Kandy, where there are numerous restaurants offering a variety of cuisines.
  • Activities:The main attraction in Peradeniya is the Royal Botanical Gardens, where visitors can explore the extensive collection of plants, trees, and flowers, as well as enjoy leisurely walks and picnics.

Places for travel

Royal Botanical Gardens is one of the oldest and largest botanical gardens in Sri Lanka, known for its diverse collection of orchids, palms, and medicinal plants. Highlights include the Avenue of Palms, the Orchid House, and the Cannonball Tree.

Matale

Location

Matale is a city located in the central hills of Sri Lanka, approximately 142 kilometers (88 miles) northeast of Colombo. It is situated in the Matale District of the Central Province.

Historical Significance

Matale has a rich history dating back to ancient times. It was an important strategic location during various periods of Sri Lankan history, including the Kandyan Kingdom and the colonial era. The area is known for its cultural heritage, spice gardens, and historical landmarks.

Tourism Infrastructure

  • Accommodation:Matale offers a range of accommodation options, including guesthouses, small hotels, and eco-lodges.
  • Dining: The city has local eateries offering traditional Sri Lankan cuisine, as well as some international dining options.
  • Activities: Visitors can enjoy activities such as visiting spice gardens, exploring historical sites and temples, hiking in the nearby hills, and experiencing cultural performances.

Places for travel

Aluvihare Rock Temple, Sembuwatta Lake, Matale Hindu Temple, Matale Cultural Triangle, Spice Gardens.
