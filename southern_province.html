SOUTHERN

Discover the Southern Province of Sri Lanka

Welcome to the Southern Province of Sri Lanka, a paradise where pristine beaches meet lush rainforests, and ancient temples stand alongside vibrant, bustling markets. Whether you're seeking a tranquil escape or an adventurous getaway, this region has something for everyone.

Explore the historic city of Galle with its iconic Dutch Fort, a UNESCO World Heritage Site that offers a glimpse into the island's colonial past. Wander through charming streets lined with colonial architecture, quaint cafes, and unique boutiques. Venture further to find the breathtaking beaches of Mirissa, Unawatuna, and Tangalle, perfect for sunbathing, surfing, and whale watching.

Don't miss a visit to Yala National Park, where you can embark on an exhilarating safari to spot leopards, elephants, and a myriad of exotic wildlife. The Southern Province is also rich in cultural heritage. Experience the spirituality of the Kataragama Temple, revered by Buddhists, Hindus, and Muslims alike, or explore the ancient rock temple of Mulkirigala.

Savor the flavors of traditional Sri Lankan cuisine, with fresh seafood and aromatic spices that will tantalize your taste buds. From bustling fish markets to serene tea plantations, the Southern Province promises an unforgettable journey filled with diverse experiences and warm hospitality. Come and discover the magic of the Southern Province,where every moment is a memory in the making.

Galle

Location

Galle is a major city on the southwestern coast of Sri Lanka, approximately 119 kilometers (74 miles) south of Colombo. It is situated in the Southern Province of the country.

Historical Significance

Galle is renowned for its rich history and colonial heritage, particularly the Galle Fort, which is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The city has been a significant port and trading hub since ancient times, with influences from Portuguese, Dutch, and British colonization.

Tourism Infrastructure

  • Accommodation: Galle offers a range of accommodation options, from luxury hotels and boutique guesthouses within the fort to budget-friendly hostels and beach resorts.
  • Dining: The city boasts diverse dining options, including local Sri Lankan cuisine, seafood specialties and international restaurants.
  • Activities: Visitors can enjoy activities such as historical tours, beach outings, snorkeling, diving, and shopping for local crafts and souvenirs.

Places for travel

Galle Fort, Galle Lighthouse, Dutch Reformed Church, National Maritime Museum, Unawatuna Beach, Japanese Peace Pagoda, Jungle Beach, Flag Rock Bastion

Matara

Location

Matara is a major city on the southern coast of Sri Lanka, approximately 160 kilometers (99 miles) south of Colombo. It is situated in the Southern Province of the country.

Historical Significance

Matara is known for its rich history and colonial heritage. The city has been an important port and trading center since ancient times. Matara features remnants of Portuguese and Dutch colonial architecture, most notably the Star Fort and Matara Fort.

Tourism Infrastructure

  • Accommodation: Matara offers a variety of accommodation options, from luxury hotels and beachfront resorts to budget-friendly guesthouses and homestays.
  • Dining: The city has a diverse culinary scene, with local Sri Lankan cuisine, fresh seafood, and international dining options available.
  • Activities: Visitors can engage in activities such as exploring historical sites, enjoying the beaches, surfing, snorkeling, whale watching, and visiting local markets.

Places for travel

Matara Fort, Star Fort, Weherahena Temple, Polhena Beach, Dondra Head Lighthouse, Parevi Duwa, Madiha Beach, Snake Farm.

Hambantota

Location

Hambantota is a prominent city on the southeastern coast of Sri Lanka, approximately 240 kilometers (149 miles) southeast of Colombo. It is situated in the Southern Province of the country.

Historical Significance

Hambantota has a rich history that dates back to ancient times when it served as a prominent port and trading hub. The city has been influenced by various colonial powers, including the Portuguese, Dutch, and British. Notable historical landmarks include the Martello Tower and the remnants of ancient Buddhist temples.

Tourism Infrastructure

  • Accommodation:Hambantota offers a range of accommodation options, from luxury resorts and eco-lodges to budget-friendly hotels and guesthouses.
  • Dining: The city provides diverse dining options, including traditional Sri Lankan cuisine, fresh seafood, and international restaurants.
  • Activities: Visitors can enjoy activities such as wildlife safaris, beach outings, bird watching, and exploring cultural and historical sites.

Places for travel

Hambantota Port, Bundala National Park, Yala National Park, Kumana National Park, Hambantota Beach, Mirijjawila Botanical Garden, Kataragama Temple, Kirinda Temple, Ridiyagama Safari Park.
