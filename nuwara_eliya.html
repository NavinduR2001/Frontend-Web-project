NUWARA ELIYA

Experience the Charm of Nuwara Eliya, Sri Lanka

Welcome to Nuwara Eliya, affectionately known as "Little England," where lush green hills, cascading waterfalls, and colonial-era charm come together to create a picturesque retreat. Nestled in the heart of Sri Lanka's hill country, Nuwara Eliya offers a refreshing escape with its cool climate and stunning landscapes.

Stroll through the beautifully manicured gardens of Victoria Park, a haven for nature lovers and bird watchers. Visit the historic Nuwara Eliya Golf Club, one of the oldest golf clubs in Asia, and enjoy a round of golf amidst breathtaking scenery. Discover the region's rich tea heritage by touring a working tea plantation and factory. Learn about the intricate process of tea production and sample some of the world's finest Ceylon tea while taking in panoramic views of verdant tea estates.

Admire the enchanting beauty of Gregory Lake, where you can enjoy boating, picnicking, and horseback riding against a backdrop of rolling hills. For adventure enthusiasts, the nearby Horton Plains National Park offers thrilling hikes to World's End, a dramatic cliff with awe-inspiring views, and Baker's Falls, a spectacular waterfall.

Nuwara Eliya's colonial architecture, charming cottages, and quaint streets transport you back in time. Visit the iconic Post Office, a Tudor-style building that stands as a testament to the town's British colonial past. Savor the unique flavors of Nuwara Eliya with its fresh produce and traditional Sri Lankan dishes. From high tea at elegant hotels to local street food, dining here is a delightful experience. Whether you're exploring botanical gardens, enjoying outdoor adventures, or simply relaxing in a serene setting, Nuwara Eliya promises an unforgettable journey filled with natural beauty and old-world charm.

Nuwara Eliya

Location

Nuwara Eliya is a city located in the central highlands of Sri Lanka, approximately 167 kilometers (104 miles) east of Colombo. It is situated in the Nuwara Eliya District of the Central Province, at an elevation of 1,868 meters (6,128 feet) above sea level.

Historical Significance

Nuwara Eliya, often referred to as "Little England," was established by British colonialists in the 19th century as a hill station to escape the tropical heat. The city retains much of its colonial charm with British-style architecture, gardens, and an old-world atmosphere. It has been a popular retreat for locals and tourists alike for its cool climate and scenic beauty.

Tourism Infrastructure

  • Accommodation: Nuwara Eliya offers a variety of accommodation options, including luxury hotels, colonial-style bungalows, guesthouses, and budget-friendly lodges.
  • Dining: The city has a diverse culinary scene, with restaurants serving traditional Sri Lankan cuisine, international dishes, and British-style afternoon teas.
  • Activities: Visitors can enjoy activities such as exploring tea plantations, hiking, visiting botanical gardens, golfing, and sightseeing tours.

Places for travel

Gregory Lake, Victoria Park, Hakgala Botanical Gardens, Pedro Tea Estate, Nuwara Eliya Golf Club, Lover's Leap Waterfall, Seetha Amman Temple.

Horton plains

Location

Horton Plains is a national park located in the central highlands of Sri Lanka, approximately 32 kilometers (20 miles) from Nuwara Eliya. It is situated in the Nuwara Eliya District of the Central Province, at an elevation of around 2,100-2,300 meters (6,900-7,500 feet) above sea level.

Historical Significance

Horton Plains has been recognized for its ecological significance and unique biodiversity. It was declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site as part of the Central Highlands of Sri Lanka in 2010. The park has historical importance as well, with evidence of prehistoric human settlements.

Tourism Infrastructure

  • Accommodation: While there are no accommodations within the park itself, nearby towns like Nuwara Eliya and Ohiya offer various lodging options, including hotels, guesthouses, and eco-lodges.
  • Dining: Visitors typically dine in nearby towns, where there are restaurants and eateries serving traditional Sri Lankan cuisine, as well as international options.
  • Activities:The primary activities in Horton Plains include hiking, wildlife watching, birdwatching, and photography. The park is famous for its scenic trails and viewpoints.

Places for travel

World's End, Baker's Falls, Mini World's End, Kirigalpotta, Totupolakanda, Horton Plains Wildlife.

Ella

Location

Ella is a small town in the Badulla District of the Uva Province, located in the central highlands of Sri Lanka. It is approximately 200 kilometers (124 miles) east of Colombo and is situated at an elevation of about 1,041 meters (3,415 feet) above sea level.

Historical Significance

Ella is known for its scenic beauty and rich history, with links to ancient legends and colonial heritage. The town is associated with the Ramayana epic, particularly the Ravana Falls and Ravana Cave. During the British colonial period, Ella was a significant location for tea plantations.

Tourism Infrastructure

  • Accommodation:Ella offers a variety of accommodation options, including luxury resorts, boutique hotels, guesthouses, and budget-friendly hostels.
  • Dining: The town has a diverse culinary scene with restaurants serving traditional Sri Lankan dishes, international cuisine, and health-conscious options.
  • Activities:Visitors can enjoy activities such as hiking, sightseeing, exploring waterfalls, visiting tea plantations, and experiencing the local culture and cuisine.

Places for travel

Ella Rock, Little Adam's Peak, Nine Arches Bridge, Ravana Falls, Ravana Cave, Diyaluma Falls, Ella Spice Garden.
