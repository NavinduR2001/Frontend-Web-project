COLOMBO

Discover the Colombo Province of Sri Lanka

Welcome to Colombo Province, the bustling heart of Sri Lanka, where modernity meets tradition in a vibrant cultural mosaic. As the island's economic and political hub, Colombo offers an array of experiences for every traveler.

Begin your journey in the capital city, Colombo, a dynamic metropolis that effortlessly blends colonial architecture with contemporary high-rises. Stroll along the scenic Galle Face Green, where you can enjoy stunning ocean views and sample street food delicacies. Discover the city's rich history at the National Museum, or explore the sacred Gangaramaya Temple, a center of Buddhist learning and worship.

Colombo's diverse neighborhoods, such as Pettah, are a shopper's paradise, with bustling markets offering everything from spices to handcrafted jewelry. Enjoy the lively atmosphere as you navigate through vibrant bazaars and discover hidden gems. For those seeking a more tranquil experience, the picturesque Mount Lavinia Beach provides a perfect escape just a short drive from the city center. Here, you can relax on golden sands, swim in the warm waters of the Indian Ocean, and savor fresh seafood at beachside restaurants.

Colombo Province is also a culinary delight, with an array of dining options that reflect its multicultural heritage. From traditional Sri Lankan curries to international cuisines, the food scene here is sure to satisfy any palate. With its blend of historical landmarks, cultural richness, and modern amenities, Colombo Province is an essential destination for any traveler looking to experience the best of Sri Lanka. Colombo

Location

Colombo is the capital and largest city of Sri Lanka, located on the western coast of the island. It is approximately 119 kilometers (74 miles) north of Galle and situated in the Western Province.

Historical Significance

Colombo has a rich and diverse history, serving as an important port city for centuries. It has been influenced by Portuguese, Dutch, and British colonization, which is evident in its architecture and cultural heritage. Key historical landmarks include the Old Parliament Building and the Dutch Hospital.

Tourism Infrastructure

  • Accommodation: Colombo offers a wide range of accommodation options, from luxury hotels and boutique guesthouses to budget-friendly hostels and serviced apartments.
  • Dining: The city boasts a vibrant culinary scene, with a variety of restaurants offering traditional Sri Lankan cuisine, fresh seafood, and international dishes.
  • Activities:Visitors can enjoy activities such as city tours, shopping, dining, cultural performances, and exploring historical sites and museums.

Places for travel

Galle Face Green, Gangaramaya Temple, Beira Lake, Independence Memorial Hall, Colombo National Museum, Viharamahadevi Park, Pettah Market, Dutch Hospital Shopping Precinct, Colombo Fort, Old Parliament Building.

Kaluthara

Location

Kalutara is a major city on the western coast of Sri Lanka, approximately 40 kilometers (25 miles) south of Colombo. It is situated in the Western Province of the country.

Historical Significance

Kalutara has a rich history that dates back to ancient times, known for its significance as a trading hub and a center of Buddhism. The city is home to the iconic Kalutara Bodhiya, one of the most revered Buddhist sites in Sri Lanka. It also features remnants of Portuguese and Dutch colonial influences.

Tourism Infrastructure

  • Accommodation: Kalutara offers a range of accommodation options, from luxury beach resorts and boutique hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses and homestays.
  • Dining: The city has a diverse culinary scene, with options including traditional Sri Lankan cuisine, seafood restaurants, and international dining.
  • Activities: Visitors can enjoy activities such as exploring historical sites, beach outings, river cruises, and visiting local markets and temples.

Places for travel

Kalutara Bodhiya, Richmond Castle, Kalutara Beach, Thudugala Ella Waterfall, Kalutara Vihara, Fa Hien Cave, Asokaramaya Buddhist Temple, Brief Garden by Bevis Bawa, Beruwala Lighthouse.

Gampaha

Location

Gampaha is a major city located in the Western Province of Sri Lanka, approximately 30 kilometers (19 miles) northeast of Colombo. It is an important urban center in the Gampaha District.

Historical Significance

Gampaha has a rich historical background with significance in both colonial and pre-colonial eras. The area is known for its ancient temples and colonial-era architecture. It was developed significantly during British colonial rule, especially with the introduction of the railway.

Tourism Infrastructure

  • Accommodation:Gampaha offers a variety of accommodation options, including boutique hotels, guesthouses, and budget-friendly lodges.
  • Dining: The city has a range of dining options, featuring traditional Sri Lankan cuisine, local eateries, and some international restaurants.
  • Activities: Visitors can enjoy activities such as exploring historical sites, visiting botanical gardens, and engaging in religious and cultural tours.

Places for travel

Henarathgoda Botanical Garden, Aththanagalla Raja Maha Viharaya, Maligatenna Raja Maha Viharaya, Horagolla National Park, Gampaha Railway Station, Asgiriya Rajamaha Viharaya, Pilikuththuwa Raja Maha Viharaya, Uththararama Purana Viharaya, Barandi Kovila.
